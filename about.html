Our History

The Wildlife Conservation Society (WCS) was founded in 1895 as the New York Zoological Society. Our mission has always been to protect wildlife and wild places. Over the years, we have grown into a global organization with programs in over 60 countries.

Milestones:

  • 1895:
    • Founded as the New York Zoological Society.
  • 1902:
    • Established the Bronx Zoo, the first zoo in the United States to breed animals in captivity
  • 1920:
    • Began conservation work in Africa, with a focus on protecting elephants and rhinos
  • 1960:
    • Expanded conservation efforts to Asia and Latin America
  • 1990:
    • Launched the 96 Elephants campaign to protect African elephants from poaching
