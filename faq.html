Frequently Asked Questions


1.What is wildlife conservation?

Wildlife conservation is the practice of protecting and preserving wildlife and their habitats to ensure their survival and well-being.

What is an endangered species?

An endangered species is a plant or animal that is at risk of becoming extinct due to habitat loss, pollution, climate change, or other human activities.

What does the Wildlife Conservation Society do?

The Wildlife Conservation Society (WCS) works to protect wildlife and wild places through conservation programs, education, and community engagement.

How can I help with wildlife conservation?

There are many ways to help with wildlife conservation, including donating to conservation organizations, volunteering, reducing your carbon footprint, and spreading awareness about conservation issues.

What are some of the biggest threats to wildlife conservation?

Some of the biggest threats to wildlife conservation include habitat loss and fragmentation, climate change, pollution, overhunting and poaching, and human-wildlife conflict.
